Research

  • (1) AI Research
    • Co-First Author: Machine learning-based models for optimizing outpatient follow-up schedules after cervical spinal laminoplasty surgery
      Published in BMC Medical Informatics and Decision Making (2024)
      https://doi.org/10.1186/s12911-024-02693-y
      Research Summary: Developed and validated a machine learning-based framework to optimize clinical follow-up schedules for patients undergoing cervical laminoplasty. Leveraged time-series patient data, including the Japanese Orthopedic Association (JOA) scores from multiple postoperative timepoints, to predict 2-year outcomes using various machine learning algorithms. Achieved the highest predictive performance (AUC 0.90 ± 0.13) with an LSTM-based model.
  • (2) AI + BCI Research
    • First Author: Functional Near-Infrared Spectroscopy Signals as Biomarkers for the Quantitative Assessment of Nausea: A Pilot Study
      Presented at the Spring Academic Conference of The Korean Society of Medical Informatics (KOSMI) (2024)
      Research Summary: Conducted a pilot study exploring the use of functional Near-Infrared Spectroscopy (fNIRS) signals as biomarkers for objectively quantifying nausea, a primary symptom of cybersickness. Achieved a significant predictive accuracy with an R² value of 0.9045.
    • First Author: Development of an EEG-Based Real-Time Attention State Classification Machine Learning Model: Enhancing Achievement in Medical VR Training
      Presented at the Korean Society of Computer Information (KSCI) Spring Academic Conference (2024)
      Award: Best Paper Award
      Research Summary: Developed a real-time EEG-based attention state classification model integrated into a VR training program for preoperative education, achieving a high performance (AUROC = 0.9336).
  • (3) BCI
    • First Author: Generating a Realistic Rolling Illusion in Immersive Virtual Reality Environment Using Galvanic Vestibular Stimulation
      Presented at the Fall Academic Conference of The Korean Society of Medical Informatics (KOSMI) (2024)
      Research Summary: Investigated the efficacy of transcranial alternating current stimulation (tACS) applied to the vestibular nerve for inducing a rolling illusion in a VR environment, enhancing the sense of presence.
